\begin{abstract}
\noindent\textbf{Motivation:} Ancestry calls for near-isogenic lines (NILs) from
low-cost sequencing normally require calibrating a caller against high-coverage
truth samples, of which few are usually available. We ask whether the calibration
can instead come from cheap simulations.\\
\textbf{Results:} We present nilHMM, a unified duration-aware three-state
(REF/HET/ALT) hidden Markov model with two swappable axes --- the emission (read
counts vs.\ a hard genotype call) and the duration (geometric vs.\ rigidity) ---
that expresses a family of ancestry callers as coordinates in one engine: nNIL,
bbNIL, RTIGER (a Julia-free port), and catiger, alongside a binned Gaussian caller
(binhmm) and an RNA caller (ATLAS). We set each caller's segmentation parameter by a
minimum-distance match to Broman \texttt{simcross} donor-fragment simulations rather
than to scarce high-coverage truth, and show the calibrated callers reconstruct
held-out simulated mosaics to ${\approx}0.99$ Dice and rank cleanly by the spatial
coherence of the association landscape they produce, the duration-aware callers
carrying ${\sim}9\times$ less high-frequency noise than a no-HMM baseline. On an
independent public RIL panel (TeoNAM) degraded to low coverage and re-scanned under a
mixed model, the duration-aware callers keep the association landscape clean where a
no-HMM control does not --- the control admits a genome-wide forest of spurious peaks
at low depth while the HMM callers stay localized. The engine further transfers across
DNA (skim WGS) and RNA ($3'$ BRB-seq) modalities on real BZea teosinte-introgression
NILs.\\
\textbf{Availability:} The \texttt{nilHMM} R package
(\url{https://sawers-rellan-labs.github.io/nilhmm/}) and the \texttt{zealhmm}
analysis repository (\url{https://sawers-rellan-labs.github.io/zealhmm/}) are
openly available; simulated benchmark data are regenerated with pinned seeds.\\
\textbf{Contact:} \href{mailto:rrellan@ncsu.edu}{rrellan@ncsu.edu}\\
\textbf{Supplementary information:} Supplementary data are available at
\textit{Bioinformatics} online.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\lettrine[lines=2]{\color{color2}N}{}ear-isogenic lines (NILs) carry small donor
introgressions on an otherwise recurrent-parent background, and mapping their
ancestry from sequencing underpins introgression and QTL studies. In the BZea
project, teosinte donor segments are introgressed into a B73 background and read
out by several sequencing modalities of differing cost and depth --- from
low-coverage skim whole-genome sequencing to $3'$ RNA-seq --- so that the same lines
are genotyped cheaply but at depths where most sites carry no read and a single-marker
call is nearly uninformative \citep{elshire_2011,nielsen_2011}. Recovering the donor
mosaic from such data is a hidden-Markov segmentation problem: linkage makes ancestry a
long-block signal, and a duration-aware HMM pools evidence across markers to call
tracts that no site resolves on its own.

The practical obstacle is not the model but its calibration. An HMM ancestry caller has
free segmentation parameters, and the established practice is to tune them against a
handful of high-coverage truth samples --- the maize nested-NIL caller of Zhong
\emph{et al.}, for instance, optimized its HMM parameters on $24$ of $888$ lines
re-genotyped on a SNP chip \citep{zhong2025}. Truth samples are exactly what
low-cost projects have fewest of, and different assays (skim DNA, RNA-seq) need
different settings, so the calibration bottleneck recurs with every modality. We ask
whether the calibration can instead come from cheap forward simulations of the breeding
design, whose ancestry is known exactly.

We present nilHMM, a single duration-aware three-state (REF/HET/ALT) HMM whose emission
and duration layers are independent, swappable modules; every named ancestry caller ---
including faithful ports of RTIGER \citep{rtiger_2023} and Holland's nNIL
\citep{zhong2025} --- is a coordinate in this two-axis engine. We show that the callers'
segmentation parameters can be calibrated from Broman \texttt{simcross} simulations
rather than from scarce truth, that the calibrated callers preserve downstream QTL
signal on an independent public RIL panel (TeoNAM) degraded to low coverage, and that
they transfer across DNA (skim WGS) and RNA (BRB-seq) modalities of the real BZea NILs.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{Materials and Methods}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\label{sec:materials:methods}

\subsection{A unified duration-aware three-state HMM engine}
One HMM over states REF/HET/ALT (Figure~\ref{fig:engine}A). Two axes are swappable
and define each caller: the \emph{emission} (count/BetaBinomial vs.\ categorical
genotype-confusion) and the \emph{duration/rigidity} layer (geometric, rigidity, or
HSMM). Design start-priors come from the pedigree (BC2S2/BC2S3). The engine is
data-agnostic: $(\text{data}, \text{params}) \rightarrow \text{calls}$. Every named
caller is a coordinate in this (emission $\times$ duration) space
(Figure~\ref{fig:engine}B), and on real BZea NILs the callers produce concordant
ancestry paint across data sources through this one engine (Figure~\ref{fig:paint}).



\subsection{The callers}
Each named caller is a coordinate in the (emission $\times$ duration) grid
(Figure~\ref{fig:engine}B); the four grid callers differ only along those two axes.
\subsubsection{nNIL} Categorical genotype-confusion emission with a geometric
duration --- Holland's original near-isogenic-line caller, on hard genotype calls
\citep{zhong2025}.
\subsubsection{bbNIL} The same geometric duration with a count/BetaBinomial
emission; the low-coverage extension of nNIL to read-count data, where the
self-transition is the smoother.
\subsubsection{RTIGER} Count/BetaBinomial emission with a rigidity (hard
minimum-run) duration in place of the free geometric one; a Julia-free port
\citep{rtiger_2023}.
\subsubsection{catiger} Categorical emission with the rigidity duration --- the
categorical$+$rigidity caller.
\subsubsection{ATLAS} An RNA caller: because expression and allele-specific
expression confound the competitive-alignment allele fraction (so the BetaBinomial
cannot model it), ATLAS thresholds the competitive counts to hard genotype calls
(GOOGA) and runs catiger --- a wrapper, GOOGA $+$ catiger \citep{flagel2019,veltsos2024}.
\subsubsection{binhmm} A three-state Gaussian emission on per-bin allele frequency
with REF anchored at the coverage floor (dense skim only); off the shared axes.
\subsubsection{External baselines and control} Two established biparental imputers
are ported verbatim as external baselines for calibration and benchmarking ---
LB-Impute \citep{lbimpute_2016}, which carries a distance-dependent recombination
decay, and FSFHap (a TASSEL parent-call plus per-family EM haplotype imputer). A
no-HMM \emph{control} --- the per-site genotype-likelihood MAP with no transition
model --- isolates what the duration prior buys and, being heterozygous-prone at low
coverage, serves as the het-excess reference the HMM callers must beat.

\subsection{Data sources and marker sets}
Skim (low-coverage WGS with teosinte--B73 wideseq filtering), BRB-seq
(3\textquotesingle{} RNA-seq, wideseq-thinned), and MolBreeding target-seq (45K,
high coverage; completeness only). For external validation we additionally use the
public TeoNAM RIL panel \citep{chen2019teonam} --- an independent design (BC1S4
recombinant inbreds, five teosinte parents $\times$ W22), dense genotypes at
51{,}544 AGPv2 SNPs, 22 phenotyped traits, and published QTL.

\subsection{The \texttt{simcross} simulation model}
Forward-simulated BC2S3 (and bulked BC2S2 for skim) NILs on the consensus
genetic map with interference, degraded to each source's depth/error regime
(coverage-driven missingness $\text{missing}(\lambda)=\pi+(1-\pi)e^{-k\lambda}$
and per-read error).

\subsection{Calibration and validation protocol}
Caller parameters are calibrated on \texttt{simcross} truth: segmentation rigidity
by minimum distance to the simulated donor-fragment distribution (not MLE), and
$\text{err}$/emission means on degraded-simulation truth. We then validate on two
tiers. \emph{(i) Simulated truth} --- held-out simulations scored by per-bin state
accuracy, segment-boundary error, breakpoint count, donor-footprint ROC, and
HET-vs-ALT calibration. \emph{(ii) Empirical truth} --- an independent public RIL
panel on which the calibrated pipeline must recover published QTL from
low-coverage-degraded real genotypes (next subsection).

\subsection{External validation on a public RIL panel (TeoNAM)}
Beyond simulated truth, we test whether the calibrated pipeline preserves
downstream mapping signal on \emph{real} genotypes. We treat the TeoNAM hardcall
dosages (0/1/2 $=$ W22\,/\,heterozygous\,/\,teosinte) as truth, lift the AGPv2
marker positions to B73 NAM v5 (v2$\rightarrow$v4$\rightarrow$v5 by chain-file
liftover) and attach cM from the consensus map, then simulate low-coverage read
counts from the true dosages with the same degradation model used for the
\texttt{simcross} benchmark (coverage-driven missingness and per-read error). Each
calibrated caller is run over a coverage sweep, and QTL are re-mapped on the
recovered genotypes with the pipeline applied to the truth, isolating the effect
of degradation and caller error. Recovery is read on the authentic
$118{,}838$-SNP Chen panel under the published mixed model --- a family fixed
effect plus a VanRaden kinship random effect (the Family$+$K analog of Chen's
Fig.~4C model). The kinship term deflates genomic inflation
($\lambda_{\mathrm{GC}}$ from $2.6$ to $1.1$) but, because teosinte ancestry is
collinear with the domestication QTL, it also absorbs the ancestry-confounded
peaks; callers are therefore compared on genome-wide false-positive control rather
than on peak power. The sweep is carried by a strongly, consistently signed
domestication trait (staminate spikelet, STAM; five QTL); a second, polygenic
flowering trait (days to anthesis, DTA) is a cross-trait check against the
published joint-linkage QTL \citep{chen2019teonam}. This is a real-genotype,
simulated-read test --- it probes the genotype$\rightarrow$degrade$\rightarrow$
call$\rightarrow$map chain under realistic recombination and trait architecture,
not raw low-coverage resequencing.

\section{Results}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\subsection{Simulation calibration}
Before calibrating against the simulated truth we confirmed that the truth generator
is itself unbiased. The \texttt{simcross} forward-simulation reproduces the
single-locus breeding-scheme genotype frequencies of the design: for the BC2S2 skim
cohort the simulated $(\mathrm{REF},\mathrm{HET},\mathrm{ALT})$ fractions
$(0.841,0.063,0.096)$ match the Mendelian expectation $(0.844,0.063,0.094)$ and the
donor dosage is $0.127$ against the invariant $0.125$; for BC2S3 a Hotelling $T^2$ on
the (HET, ALT) genome fractions --- with the sample, not the marker, as the unit of
replication --- does not reject the design expectation ($T^2=0.47$, $p=0.79$), all
three state frequencies falling inside their $95\%$ intervals. A companion
marker-resolution sweep shows the only measurement bias is a slight HET undercount at
coarse marker spacing that becomes negligible ($|d|<0.05$) below ${\sim}0.5$\,Mb, well
inside the working grid.

Each caller's single segmentation hyperparameter is then set by a two-stage search on
this truth --- a coarse log-spaced sweep to bracket the optimum, then a golden-section
refinement --- maximizing the donor-fragment Dice against the known mosaic
(Figure~\ref{fig:calib}). The refined optima are $\texttt{rrate}^\star=1.1\times10^{-3}$
(bbNIL), $\texttt{rigidity}^\star=2$ markers (RTIGER), and
$\texttt{recombdist}^\star=5.5$\,cM (LB-Impute). The objective is a minimum-distance
match to the true fragment-size distribution, not a likelihood of the parameter: each
hyperparameter is a low-pass filter whose cutoff can be raised without bound, so
marker-level recall saturates near $0.998$ across the whole sweep and rewards
over-fragmentation, whereas donor-fragment Dice peaks and then collapses as the filter
is turned up (bbNIL $0.97\rightarrow0.21$ with the breakpoint count exploding from
${\sim}6{,}200$ to ${\sim}34{,}000$ against ${\sim}6{,}900$ true breakpoints; RTIGER
$0.97\rightarrow0.76$ from rigidity $2$ to $128$). The intermediate optimum is the
bias--variance trade-off at the biological fragment scale, which is why the
calibrated cutoff --- not maximal smoothness --- is the setting at which callers are
compared.

\subsection{Simulation benchmark and caller ranking}
Scored on a held-out simulated cohort at their calibrated settings, all three
duration-aware callers reconstruct the donor mosaic near-perfectly: marker-level
macro-Dice is ${\approx}0.99$ for each, and on the stricter donor-fragment metric
LB-Impute leads ($\mathrm{Dice}=0.979$, $\mathrm{FDR}=0.006$), with RTIGER
($0.967$, $0.013$) and bbNIL ($0.966$, $0.011$) close behind and all recovering the
${\sim}6{,}900$ true breakpoints to within a few percent
(fragment-size $\mathrm{KS}\approx0.02$). The three are therefore near-equivalent on
raw reconstruction accuracy at skim depth; they separate on the \emph{spatial
quality} of the association landscape they produce.

We score that landscape geometry directly. Reading a $-\log_{10}P$ track as a spatial
stochastic process, a genuine QTL produces autocorrelated evidence at the inherited
ancestry-tract scale, whereas caller error injects high-frequency noise; we therefore
measure the fraction of spectral power in the sub-tract noise band (between the grid
resolution $R\approx77$\,kb and the $10$th-percentile resolvable tract
$P_{10}\approx181$\,kb) and the lag-one autocorrelation of each caller's scan
(Table~\ref{tab:ranking}). The duration-aware HMMs are an order of magnitude cleaner
than the no-HMM baselines: binhmm and RTIGER carry ${\sim}0.0005$ of their power in
the noise band ($\mathrm{acf}_1\approx0.999$) versus ${\sim}0.018$ for the control and
bbNIL ($\mathrm{acf}_1\approx0.96$) --- a ${\sim}9\times$ difference, with the two
spectral lenses ranking the callers in exactly reverse order as the
Wiener--Khinchin duality requires. Crucially the smoothest caller is not blindly
preferred: because each caller is scored at its fragment-scale calibrated cutoff (not
at maximal smoothness), coherence tracks reconstruction accuracy rather than
substituting for it --- the recovery-versus-smoothing distinction the coverage sweep
tests next.

\subsection{QTL recovery on a public RIL panel (TeoNAM)}
The decisive test is whether the simulation-calibrated callers preserve a clean
association scan on an independent \emph{real} panel as coverage falls. We degrade the
real TeoNAM truth genotypes across the sweep, re-call ancestry with each caller, and
re-scan STAM on the authentic $118{,}838$-SNP Chen panel under the published mixed
model (Family$+$K), comparing against the same scan on the truth
(Figure~\ref{fig:stam-sweep}). Because the kinship term absorbs the ancestry-collinear
domestication QTL (Methods), every caller loses most of the \textit{tb1} peak height,
so the panels no longer separate on QTL power --- they separate on how much spurious,
genome-wide signal each caller admits, a direct readout of the recovered-ancestry
geometry.

The no-HMM control (panel A) fails this test: it produces a dense forest of spurious
associations across all ten chromosomes, dozens clearing $\mathrm{LOD}=5$ (to
$-\log_{10}P\approx16$), worst at the intermediate coverages where the
genotype-likelihood scatter is largest and the mixed model has no coherent block
structure to correct. The duration-aware HMM callers instead keep the scan clean and
localized across the entire sweep: RTIGER (C) and binhmm (E) are the cleanest --- little
survives threshold beyond a modest \textit{tb1} cluster (chr1) and the chr3
domestication region --- with bbNIL (B) intermediate and LB-Impute (D) close to RTIGER.
This is the same ordering as the band-limited noise metric (Table~\ref{tab:ranking}):
the duration-aware callers carry ${\sim}9\times$ less high-frequency power than the
no-HMM control and geometric-only bbNIL, and the two spectral lenses rank them in
reverse. The one residual HMM artifact is a broad chr3-pericentromeric elevation at
$0.1\times$ (strongest for the count callers), where poor kinship estimation and block
recovery in a low-recombination region let structure leak through at the shallowest
depth. A polygenic second trait (DTA) reproduces the pattern: the mixed model likewise
removes the ancestry-collinear flowering peaks (\textit{ZmCCT10}, \textit{ZCN8}) and the
callers separate on the identical cleanliness ranking (RTIGER${\,\approx\,}$binhmm
cleanest, bbNIL noisiest), so the false-positive-control result is not specific to STAM.

\subsection{Application to the paired BRB + skim cohort}
As a cross-modality application we compare skim WGS and $3'$ RNA-seq (BRB-seq) on a
pedigree-matched cohort of $334$ BZea NILs genotyped by both assays (skim mean
$0.43\times$, BRB-seq mean $2.8\times$; the two depths uncorrelated across NILs), so the
paired design isolates the modality from the sample. On the same skim reads the count
HMM and the RTIGER port agree closely (marker concordance $0.905$), and among skim
callers RTIGER and the binned Gaussian caller agree most tightly ($0.945$), so the
skim donor footprint is essentially caller-independent. Across modalities skim-RTIGER
and BRB-RTIGER concord at $0.827$ over the whole cohort, and per-NIL donor Jaccard
reaches $0.79$ where the pedigrees genuinely correspond --- the two assays recover the
same teosinte-introgression landscape (Figure~\ref{fig:brbskim}).

The one caller that does \emph{not} transfer unchanged to BRB-seq is the count HMM
with fixed emission means: RNA read depth is reference-biased and expression-scaled, so
a donor-homozygous alt-fraction sits near $0.5$ rather than $1$, and a fixed
$\theta_{\mathrm{ALT}}\approx1$ collapses true donor-homozygous tracts into heterozygous calls
(BRB donor genome fraction ${\approx}0.04$ vs.\ ${\approx}0.17$ for skim). This is
precisely the regime the engine's EM-fittable emission means and the categorical ATLAS
route are designed for, and re-running the RNA caller with fitted means (rather than the
provisional fixed-mean run shown here) is the natural next step; we present the paired
comparison as the current cross-modality evidence, with the calibrated-RNA update
pending. Chromosome painting appears only as a supplementary QC illustration.

\section{Discussion}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
Cheap simulations can stand in for the scarce high-coverage truth samples that
ancestry callers are normally calibrated against. Setting each caller's segmentation
parameter by a minimum-distance match to the \texttt{simcross} donor-fragment
distribution --- rather than by maximizing a likelihood or a smoothness score --- gives
values that reconstruct held-out simulated truth to ${\approx}0.99$ marker-Dice and,
more importantly, carry over to an \emph{independent} real panel: on TeoNAM degraded
across coverage and re-scanned under the published mixed model, the duration-aware
callers keep the association landscape clean and localized down to $0.1\times$, whereas
the no-HMM control admits a genome-wide forest of spurious peaks --- the same
false-positive ordering the band-limited noise metric predicts. That the parameters
chosen on simulation carry over to real genotypes, and that the fragment-scale
calibration --- not maximal smoothing --- is what tracks landscape cleanliness, together
support the central claim that simulation calibration is a sufficient substitute for
truth-sample calibration.

The approach has real limits. Per-site zygosity is not recoverable at skim depth for
fundamental reasons --- a rare-allele prior forces lone alternate reads to
heterozygous, and outbred-donor dilution compresses the state-conditional alt-fractions
--- so the callers infer \emph{tracts}, not site genotypes, and no emission model lifts
that ceiling. The RNA route (ATLAS/count HMM on BRB-seq) needs EM-fitted emission means
because expression scaling and reference bias move the donor-homozygous alt-fraction off
its textbook value; fixed means mis-call it, as the paired-cohort comparison shows.
High-coverage target-seq is too sparse in truth samples to calibrate \emph{from} but is
useful as a completeness cross-check. And the TeoNAM validation is a
real-genotype/simulated-read test: it exercises the genotype$\rightarrow$degrade$
\rightarrow$call$\rightarrow$map chain under realistic recombination and trait
architecture, but not the full noise of real low-coverage resequencing.

In practice the engine makes the choice of caller a choice of data channel. Skim DNA
read counts take the BetaBinomial callers (bbNIL, or RTIGER when a hard minimum run is
wanted); competitive/ASE RNA counts, whose allele fraction is expression-confounded,
take the categorical route (ATLAS $=$ GOOGA $+$ catiger); and saturated hard genotypes
collapse to the categorical nNIL at lower cost. The cross-modality application
generalizes this on real BZea NILs, with a calibrated-RNA update the natural next step,
and phenotype-driven applications such as $B1$ anthocyanin mapping follow directly once
the trait panels are assembled.
